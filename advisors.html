James Weil

James W. Weil is a private investor. Previously, Mr. Weil was a Managing Director at C.E. Unterberg, Towbin where he was responsible for hedge funds including UT Technology Partners and CEUT Select Technology Partners from 1996 to 2006. From 1991 to 1996 Mr. Weil served as a research analyst and Director of Research at C.E. Unterberg, Towbin. Matthew Luckett

Matthatthew Luckett is a seasoned alternative investor, with direct experience in hedge funds, venture capital, private equity and real estate. He is currently the Managing Partner of ALBA Holdings, a family office based in New York. ALBA Holdings is focused on direct and fund investments across all asset classes. Until June 2014, Mr. Luckett was a General Partner of Balestra Capital, LTD, a multi-billion dollar global macro investment firm. He joined the firm in 2004 as a Co-Portfolio Manager of Balestra Capital Partners (BCP), the firm’s global macro hedge fund and served on the investment committee of Balestra Spectrum Partners (BSP), the firm’s fund of funds. Mr. Luckett was the managing partner of the firm’s Management Committee and was responsible for portfolio strategy, macro-economic research and idea generation and portfolio risk supervision for Balestra Capital Partners. During his tenure, Balestra Capital Partners was ranked as one of the best performing macro funds in the world with average rates of return in excess of twenty percent per annum.

Prior to Balestra Capital, Mr. Luckett was a Principal and Senior Equity Research Analyst at SoundView Technology Group (NASDAQ: SNDV), where he was responsible for research coverage of software, Internet and business services companies. In 2000, he was named to the Wall Street Journal’s “Best on the Street” research analyst team. Upon successful sale of the firm to Wit Capital and subsequently to Charles Schwab, he became a venture capitalist and a senior operating executive for several portfolio companies. Mr. Luckett began his career as an industry analyst at Gartner Group, where he helped launch two multi-million dollar research practices, advising Fortune 500 enterprises on software licensing and pricing, as well as researching emerging trends in enterprise software and data analytics.

Mr. Luckett was a George F. Baker Scholar at Georgetown University where he graduated with honors. He holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in American Studies with a minor in Japanese. He currently serves on the Georgetown College Board of Advisors and previously served on the Board of Trustees for the Baker Scholars program. Mr. Luckett has been on the board of directors of a broad spectrum of for-profit and non-profit organizations. He is an accomplished marathoner and a nationally ranked age-group triathlete, reaching All America status multiple times and competing for Team USA at the 2014 Age Group World Championships. He lives in New York City with his wife, Liz, and their two children.

Christy Sadler

Managing Partner & Founder

Mr. Sadler has over 30 years of experience in mergers & acquisitions advisory and private equity/leveraged buyouts. A significant portion of this experience was spent with companies in the building industry, including roofing fastener manufacturing, hard wood flooring manufacturing and tile, stone and decorative glass distribution industries.

In addition to being a Managing Partner at Anchor Peabody, Mr. Sadler is the founder and owner of Willowmere Capital Partners. This investment firm invests private capital in public and private debt and equity securities.

Mr. Sadler has been a General Partner at various private equity/buyout firms including Palladium Equity Partners, Saugatuck Capital Company and Dubin Clark & Company. Mr. Sadler began his career at General Electric Company and spent 5 years in the Mergers & Acquisition Department of KPMG Peat Marwick.

Mr. Sadler has an MBA in Finance from the Columbia University Graduate School of Business and a BA in Urban Studies from Brown University.

Matthew Lamberti

Mr. Lamberti is currently the Director of Alternatives at North Sound Management, a single family office located in Greenwich, CT. At North Sound he runs an alternative investment portfolio which invests in hedge funds, short duration private equity, co-investment opportunities and select direct investments on behalf of its principle.

Prior to joining North Sound Management, Mr. Lamberti worked at Balestra Capital in New York where he was the sole research analyst on a 200+mm fund of hedge funds portfolio. That portfolio invested in both external managers and in liquid market securities.

Prior to Balestra Capital, Mr. Lamberti worked at a fund of hedge funds called Ranger Advisors in Dallas, a quantitative equity market neutral hedge fund named Gamma Advisors in Philadelphia, and a leading institutional consulting firm called NEPC in Boston.

Mr. Lamberti received his B.S. in Finance from Oral Roberts University in 2001 and holds the CFA designation and CAIA designation. He lives with his wife and 3 kinds in Armonk, NY.

Steve Kirkpatrick

Steve Kirkpatrick began his career in investment management and investment banking with Robertson Stephens & Company. He subsequently co-founded and co-managed Discovery Capital Partners, a long-short equity hedge fund focused on small and micro-cap companies, delivering positive returns in a negative market environment. As a Managing Director of Glass Lewis & Co, Steve worked with accounting and legal professionals and the firm’s clients to develop tools and research services to help investors, bankers, regulators and accountants identify signs of financial, fiduciary and business risk at public companies. Most recently, Steve was the CFO and Head of Business Development at Focal Upright, an award-winning ergonomic furniture company until the company’s acquisition in mid-2016. Steve earned a BA in Economics at Tufts University and an MBA with concentrations in Finance and Marketing at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.

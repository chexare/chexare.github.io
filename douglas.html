About


The McKeige Law Firm, LLC is a first class law firm that advises clients regarding potential fraud claims and avenues for case prosecution. There are many choices and decisions that investors must make when confronted with colorable claims for investor losses. Claims may or may not have merit. Cases can be brought as class actions or individually, under federal and/or state law, or in a foreign country. Mckeige Law uses rigorous investigation and case analysis to help clients decide which cases to bring and which not to bring. A law firm that will put its client’s interests first and efficiently and successfully help them navigate these choices is indispensable.



Misconduct


There is an insatiable drive for profits, wealth and power, too often without regard to what is right, or legal.

Losses often go unaddressed.

Fiduciary responsibilities are ignored. Regulators are ineffective. Bad actors take the money and deal with possible consequences later.

No matter how talented and disciplined an investor might be – losses due to fraud and other misconduct are an unfortunate fact of life.

Recovering Losses


Lawsuits are necessary, inevitable and economic. Decision makers must assess and sometimes pursue their legal options. How? In what court? Fact investigation? Claims? Defense? Damages? Recoverability? With which counsel? On a contingent, “litigation funded” or pay by the hour basis?

Douglas McKeige, Principal


Douglas McKeige brings unparalleled knowledge, experience and talent to firm clients to investigate, assess, commence and prosecute meritorious claims for investor and other losses. Doug built and was co-managing partner of the most highly regarded and significant plaintiff securities litigation practice in the U.S. He and his team vetted hundreds of potential claims for the clients of the firm from the mid-1990s to the mid-2000s. These clients included public pension funds, hedge funds, private equity firms, high net worth investors and, equally important, hardworking Americans saving for their retirements. Utilizing his extensive knowledge of securities markets and market participants along with deep research and analysis of each situation, Doug counseled clients regarding potential claims and avenues for case prosecution. Ultimately the firm and its clients commenced and prosecuted more than 50 cases in federal and state courts throughout the U.S. that were underwritten and commenced by Mr. McKeige’s New Matter team. The cases were litigated to successful conclusions with an aggregate value in excess of $12 Billion.

Today, Doug combines his two-decade record of legal experience with a six-year record as a hedge fund Managing Director helping to grow two billion-dollar-plus hedge funds. He had daily experience with macroeconomic themes, company specific opportunities and trade implementation across all asset classes (equities, fixed income, foreign exchange and commodities), long and short, and using derivatives across all major geographies.

Mr. McKeige earned his B.A. in economics at Tufts University, cum laude and his J.D. from Tulane Law School, magna cum laude, Order of the Coif. Doug was articles editor of the Tulane Law Review. He is admitted to the Bar of the State of New York and the federal courts of the Southern and Eastern Districts of New York.
